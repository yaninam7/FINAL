Humans

Human activities contribute to climate change by gauging changes in the earth's atmosphere in the amounts of greenhouse gases , aerosol (which are small particles) and cloudiness. The largest known contribution comes from burning fossil fuel , which releases carbon dioxide to the atmosphere. Human activities result in emission of four principal greenhouse gases which are carbon dioxide (CO2) methane (CH4) , nitrous oxide (N20) and the halocarbons. Halocarbons are a group of gases containing fluorine chlorine and bromie. These gases accumulate in the atmosphere , causing concentrations to increase with time. Significant increase in all these gases have occurred in the industrial era.
